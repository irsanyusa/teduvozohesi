Profession: Explorer and Seafarer Biography: Abel Janszoon Tasman was a Dutch explorer renowned for his voyages in 1642 and 1644 under the aegis of the Dutch East India Company (VOC). Born in 1603 in the Netherlands, he became a notable navigator and contributed to the exploration of the Southern Pacific, discovering regions such as Tasmania and New Zealand. Tasman's seafaring career commenced with work as a merchant seaman, after which he joined the VOC and voyaged to Batavia (present-day Jakarta).
