    Your adorable cat and loyal dog miss you even more than you miss them when you’re away from home. So a pet camera installed in your home that lets you see them snoozing by the front door, lets them know that you’re coming home (and to stop clawing at the carpet) and even tosses them their favorite treat is worth every penny.

    The ability to virtually “drop in” on your pet anytime from anywhere you have a Wi-Fi signal is reassuring, and finding the best pet camera is really about choosing the right model that works for your unique home, lifestyle, and animals.

    How I selected the best pet cameras

    Comprehensive research of the models and brands on the market, extensive testing, and years of interacting with my own cat and dog with these fun and convenient devices led me to select this year’s best pet cameras. There are many important aspects I considered when narrowing my list down to the greatest pet cameras you can get to day, including:

    Model’s feature list

    I considered the quality of two-way audio which allows you to both hear and talk to your pet remotely. Along with fun, yet vital, extras like treat dispensers to pamper and reward your pet; interactive toys that allow you to play with your pet while way; along with a wide field of vision so you can watch your pet even when they walk out of the video frame.

    Video resolution

    Crisp and clear video quality—indoors and outdoors—makes a big difference in user experience. I don’t recommend buying a pet camera with less than a 1080p (Full HD) resolution, so every camera on this list has 1080p or 2K resolution. As I noted above, 2K higher resolution (and higher )pet cameras offer stellar picture quality, but will usually cost you more than their Full HD counterparts.

    Smart home platform integration

    There are so many high-tech pet cameras on the market, but they will do you little good if it is not compatible with your smartphone or smart home platform. I chose cameras that are compatible with a wide variety of devices and smart home platforms so you can easily connect with what you already have.

    Price

    Like many pet owners, I tend to splurge on accessories for my pets, whether it’s a decked-out pet carrier or self cleaning litter box. And while higher end pet cameras offer many fun features and top-notch quality, you can still find great pet cameras without breaking the bank. I picked household and lesser-known brands in a variety of price points from $25 to $250 to meet all budgets and needs.

    What to consider when buying a pet camera

    Resolution

    Since you’ll want a crisp and clear video feed when you check in on your pet, don’t settle for anything less than a 1080p (Full HD) resolution. Note that 2K and higher resolution pet cameras exist and provide a much clearer picture, but tend to be pricier than their Full HD counterparts.

    Features

    There are plenty of fun and exciting features built into the best pet cameras that you and your animal friends will appreciate. These include the ability to launch a treat to get their attention, a moving toy-on-a-string or laser pointer for entertaining cats, and a free-rolling design that will follow your pets around the house that continuously keeps tabs on them. Since each animal is different, it’s worth comparing models to find a pet camera that includes a list of features that your special puppy or kitty will get the most use from.

    Compatibility

    No matter how many great features a pet camera may have, it’s useless to you if it’s incompatible with your smartphone or smart home platform. Ensure your pet camera will work with your devices before you buy.

    Field of view and motion detection

    When they aren’t peacefully napping, pets move from their food bowl, to their toys, to another place to snooze when you’re away, so it’s critical to find a pet camera with a wide field of view so you’ll always be able to keep an eye on them.

    The best pet cameras have a 360-degree swivel feature so they can scan the entire room, as well as a continuous motion tracking feature that’ll keep its focus on your pet, ensuring that they’ll always be in view when you activate the camera.

    Two-way audio

    While it’s always nice to see your pet going about their day, nothing is as heartwarming as speaking directly to them through a pet camera’s two-way audio. It’s fun to watch and listen in as they get excited to hear your voice.

    What is the difference between pet cameras and home security cameras?

    While some security and surveillance cameras work well as pet cameras, these two types of devices have unique features that differentiate each other:

    Pet cameras are literally made to keep an eye on your animals when you’re away from home. These specialty cameras include pet-themed features such as a treat dispenser, two-way audio so you can talk to your pet while on the go, a swiveling 360-degree base so you can keep them in frame as long as they’re in the same room, and night or low-light vision so you can clearly see your pets after sundown.

    On the other hand, security cameras tend to be made to withstand temperature extremes, rain, and an all-around sturdy design for easily surviving being installed outside. These cameras are often pricier than the average pet camera, but without the fun interactive features that keep animals and their owners connected like treat dispensers and laser pointers.

    Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

    How much should I spend on a pet camera?

    Although pet cameras range anywhere from $30 to over $200, I recommend spending what you need to for the features you want. For instance, there are many inexpensive models on the market, but if you want to reassure your pet with your voice, give them a treat to make their day a little more fun, or a sharper resolution, it’s always worth spending extra for a superior pet monitoring system.

    Generally speaking, indoor cameras tend to be more affordable than their far more durable outdoor counterparts, partly because most outdoor cameras are packed with additional security-related features and a higher video resolution.

    How do you train your pet to react to a pet camera?

    It’s easier than you may expect! Many pet cameras include two-way audio, and your loyal pet will come running when they hear your voice coming from the camera. In addition, some models include a treat container that dispenses a snack with a tap of your smartphone, making it even easier for your animal buddy to get excited to interact with your new camera. A few editions sport dangling toys, long streaming feathers, and integrated laser pointers, making them perfect for grabbing and keeping a pet’s attention.

    Are pet cameras worth it?

    The relatively low investment cost and time spent setting up a pet camera is absolutely worth the peace of mind that comes with being able to check in on your cat, dog, or other animal at a moment’s notice.

    Pets get lonely when you’re gone, so the ability to see them going about their day, speak to them and watch them light up with excitement, and fling them a treat to break up the boredom of spending time without you is priceless.

    Do you need Wi-Fi for a pet camera?

    While many pet cameras use a Wi-Fi connection to livestream video to your smartphone, some models record footage on a hard drive or SD card to be viewed later.
